DAY 01 To Be — Positive, Negative & Questions to be ▼
📐 Formula
Positive Subject + am / is / are + ...
Negative Subject + am not / isn't / aren't + ...
Question Am / Is / Are + Subject + ...?
Who uses what? I → am    He/She/It → is    You/We/They → are
💬 Examples
+
I am a student.Men talabaman.
+
She is a doctor.U (ayol) doktordir.
+
They are happy.Ular xursand.
−
I am not tired.Men charchamadim.
−
He isn't at home.U uyda emas.
?
Are you a teacher?Siz o'qituvchimisiz?
?
Is she married?U turmushga chiqqanmi?
💡 Key Tip
Contractions: In speaking, we always use short forms: I'm, he's, she's, it's, we're, you're, they're. He is not → he isn't. Practice saying them fast!

DAY 02 Present Simple — Habits & Facts present simple ▼
📐 Formula
Positive Subject + V1 (he/she/it → V1+s) + ...
Negative Subject + don't / doesn't + V1 + ...
Question Do / Does + Subject + V1 + ...?
🔑 Key Words
alwaysusuallyoften sometimesrarelynever every dayevery week
💬 Examples
+
I work every day.Men har kuni ishlayman.
+
She drinks coffee every morning.U har ertalab qahva ichadi.
−
He doesn't eat meat.U go'sht yemaydi.
−
We don't live in London.Biz Londonda yashamaymiz.
?
Do you like music?Siz musiqani yoqtirasizmi?
?
Does she speak English?U ingliz tilida gapladimi?
💡 Key Tip
The S rule: Add -s or -es only for He / She / It in positive sentences. But in questions and negatives, use does — never add -s to the verb: ✅ Does she work? ❌ Does she works?

DAY 03 Modal Verbs — can, must, should, may modal verbs ▼
📐 Formula
All modals — same formula: Subject + modal + V1 (base) + ...
Subject + modal + not + V1 + ...
Modal + Subject + V1 + ...?

⚠ NEVER add -s, -ed, or -ing after a modal!
📚 Modal Meanings
can → ability / possibility (qila olmoq)
must → strong obligation (shart / kerak)
should → advice (kerak / maslahat)
may → possibility / polite permission (ehtimol / ruxsat)
could → past of can / polite request
💬 Examples
+
I can swim.Men suzа olaman.
+
You should study more.Ko'proq o'qishing kerak.
+
She must see a doctor.U shifokorga borishi shart.
−
He cannot drive.U mashina hayday olmaydi.
−
You shouldn't smoke.Siz chekmasligingiz kerak.
?
Can you help me?Menga yordam bera olasizmi?
?
May I come in?Kirsa bo'ladimi?
📝 Homework
✏️ Homework — Day 3

3

DAY 04 Present Continuous — Right Now & Near Future present continuous ▼
📐 Formula
Positive Subject + am/is/are + V-ing + ...
Negative Subject + am not/isn't/aren't + V-ing + ...
Question Am/Is/Are + Subject + V-ing + ...?
🔑 Key Words
nowright nowat the moment currentlytodaylook!listen!
💬 Examples
+
I am studying English now.Hozir ingliz tilini o'qiyapman.
+
She is talking on the phone.U telefonda gaplashyapti.
−
They aren't sleeping now.Ular hozir uxlamayapti.
?
Are you coming to the party?Ziyofatga kelasizmi?
?
What is he doing?U nima qilyapti?
💡 Key Tip
Stative verbs are NOT used in continuous! Never say: ❌ "I am knowing" / "She is wanting". Use simple instead: ✅ "I know" / "She wants". Stative verbs: know, want, need, love, hate, believe, understand, have (possession).

DAY 06 Have to / Has to — Obligation & Necessity have to / has to ▼
📐 Formula
Positive I/You/We/They + have to + V1
He/She/It + has to + V1
Negative don't have to / doesn't have to + V1 (= not necessary)
Question Do I have to...? / Does she have to...?
💬 Examples
+
I have to wake up early.Men erta turgunim kerak.
+
She has to work on weekends.U dam olish kunlari ishlashi kerak.
−
You don't have to come. (optional!)Kelishingiz shart emas.
?
Do you have to wear a uniform?Forma kiyishingiz shart?
💡 Key Tip
must vs. have to: Both mean obligation, but must is often a personal rule / internal obligation; have to is an external rule. "I must study" (I want to) vs. "I have to study" (the teacher says so).

DAY 10 Past Continuous — Actions In Progress past continuous ▼
📐 Formula
Positive Subject + was/were + V-ing + ...
Negative Subject + wasn't/weren't + V-ing + ...

🔑 Key Pattern
Classic combo: Past Continuous + Past Simple
"I was cooking when she arrived."
→ Was cooking = long background action  |  arrived = short interrupting action
💬 Examples

+
They were playing football when it rained.Yomg'ir yoqqanda ular futbol o'ynayotgan edi.
−
She wasn't listening to me.U meni eshitmayotgan edi.
?

DAY 11 Past Simple vs Past Continuous — Comparison comparison ▼
🔄 Key Difference
Past Simple = action is finished, we care about the event
→ "She called me at 9 PM."

Past Continuous = action was in progress at that moment
→ "She was studying at 9 PM."
💬 Contrast Examples
+
I read a book last night. (I finished it)Kecha kechqurun kitob o'qidim.
+
I was reading a book when you called. (in progress)Siz qo'ng'iroq qilganingizda kitob o'qiyotgan edim.
+
While she was cooking, he watched TV.U ovqat pishirayotganda, u televizor ko'rdi.

DAY 15 Present Perfect Simple — Experience & Result present perfect ▼
📐 Formula
Positive Subject + have/has + V3 (past participle) + ...
Negative Subject + haven't/hasn't + V3 + ...
Question Have/Has + Subject + V3 + ...?
🔑 Key Words
everneverjust alreadyyetrecently beforeso far
💬 Examples
+
I have visited Paris.Men Parijga borgam (hayotimda).
+
She has just finished her homework.U endi uyini tugatdi.
−
I haven't seen that movie yet.Men hali o'sha filmni ko'rmaganman.
?
Have you ever eaten sushi?Hech sushi yeganmisiz?
?
Has he called yet?U hali qo'ng'iroq qildimi?
💡 Key Tip
Present Perfect ≠ Past Simple: Use Present Perfect when the TIME is not mentioned or not important. Use Past Simple when you say when it happened.
✅ "I have eaten sushi." (experience, ever in life)
✅ "I ate sushi yesterday." (specific time = Past Simple)

📐 for vs. since
for = duration (length of time)

since = starting point

DAY 17 Past Perfect Simple — The Earlier Past past perfect simple ▼
📐 Formula
Positive Subject + had + V3 (past participle) + ...
Negative Subject + hadn't + V3 + ...
Question Had + Subject + V3 + ...?
🔑 When to use it
Use Past Perfect for an action that happened before another past action. It's "the past before the past."

"When I arrived, she had already left."
→ First she left → then I arrived. Both are past, but leaving happened first.
💬 Examples
+
I had eaten before he arrived.U kelmasdan oldin ovqatlanib bo'lgan edim.
+
She had never seen snow before that day.U o'sha kunga qadar hech qachon qor ko'rmagandi.
−
They hadn't finished when we got there.Biz borgunimizcha ular tugatmagandi.
?
Had you met him before the party?Ziyofatdan oldin u bilan uchrashgan edingizmi?
📝 Homework
✏️ Homework — Day 17

DAY 18 Past Perfect Continuous — Duration Before a Past Moment past perfect continuous ▼
📐 Formula
Positive Subject + had been + V-ing + ...
Negative Subject + hadn't been + V-ing + ...
Question Had + Subject + been + V-ing + ...?
💬 Examples
+
I had been waiting for 2 hours when she arrived.U kelganida men 2 soat kutgan edim.
+
He had been working all night, so he was tired.U butun kecha ishlagan, shuning uchun charchagan edi.
?
Had they been studying long?Ular uzoq o'qishganmidi?
💡 Key Tip
Past Perfect Continuous emphasizes the duration of an action that continued up to a past moment. It often answers "how long?" and explains a past state or feeling.
"He was tired because he had been running for an hour."

DAY 22 Future Simple — will & going to future simple ▼
📐 Formula
will — spontaneous decisions / predictions Subject + will + V1 + ...
Subject + won't + V1 + ...
Will + Subject + V1 + ...?

going to — plans & intentions Subject + am/is/are going to + V1 + ...
🔑 will vs. going to
will: spontaneous decision at the moment of speaking, offers, promises, predictions without evidence.
"I'll help you!" (I decided just now)

going to: a plan decided before, something you can see is about to happen.
"I'm going to study tonight." (I already planned this)
💬 Examples
+
I will call you tomorrow.Men ertaga sizga qo'ng'iroq qilaman.
+
She is going to start a new job.U yangi ish boshlaydi (rejasi bor).
−
I won't be late, I promise!Kechikmayman, va'da beraман!
?
Will you help me?Menga yordam berasizmi?
?
Are you going to travel this summer?Bu yoz sayohat qilmoqchimisiz?

DAY 23 Future Continuous — Actions In Progress in the Future future continuous ▼
📐 Formula
Positive Subject + will be + V-ing + ...
Negative Subject + won't be + V-ing + ...
Question Will + Subject + be + V-ing + ...?
🔑 When to use it
Future Continuous shows an action that will be in progress at a specific moment in the future.

"At 8 PM tomorrow, I will be studying."
→ At that moment, I will be in the middle of studying.
💬 Examples
+
This time tomorrow, I will be flying to London.Ertaga bu vaqtda Londonga uchib boraman.
+
At 9 AM, she will be giving a presentation.Soat 9 da u taqdimot qilyotgan bo'ladi.
−
Don't call at noon — I won't be working.Peshin vaqtda qo'ng'iroq qilmang — ishlamayotgan bo'laman.
?
Will you be using the car tonight?Bugun kechqurun mashinadan foydalanasizmi?

DAY 24 Future Tenses — All 3 Ways to Talk About Future future comparison ▼
🔄 Three Ways to Express Future
will + V1 → instant decision, prediction, promise
"I'll have the salad." (just decided at restaurant)

going to + V1 → planned intention, evidence-based prediction
"Look at those clouds — it's going to rain!"

will be + V-ing → action in progress at future moment, polite inquiry
"I'll be working at 5 PM."

Present Continuous → fixed arrangements (tickets bought, meetings booked)
"I'm flying to Paris on Friday." (booked!)
